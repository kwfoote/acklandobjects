Great observations. I am indeed a woman and my friend is a man. We embody and express different types of power in the Yoruba tradition. You might have noticed that he is holding weapons - a sword and a spear. He is associated with the overt power of war and the hunt. I am holding a child and am associated with the power of reproduction and nurturing. These powers are traditionally associated with our genders - male and female - and Yoruba culture often prizes complementary pairings. Ideas of balance and symmetry are important. Look at us. Where else do you see examples of balance?
