Yeah, you're right. I'm called a study because Johnson painted me in preparation to make a larger, more finished painting. He probably painted me outside, in front of this scene. He made at least thirteen other studies of cranberry picking. Here, he was probably most interested in capturing the composition and the play of light and shadow over the landscape. for instance, how the standing figures cast long shadows on the ground.
